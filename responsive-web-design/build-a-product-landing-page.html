Marlboro

American brand of cigarettes owned and manufactured by Philip Morris USA (a branch of Altria) within the United States and by Philip Morris International (PMI, now separate from Altria) in most global territories outside the US.

Features

Nicotine Delivery

A notable feature is the use of ammonia to freebase nicotine, which allows for a more efficient and powerful delivery of nicotine to the smoker. This process has been described as creating "crack tobacco" by some experts.

Crafted Variants

The brand has also introduced "crafted" or "artisanal" variants in some markets, such as Indonesia and Mexico, which feature different pack designs, a stylized sans-serif font, and sometimes a hand signature to suggest quality.

Brand Differentiation

Marlboro has also strategically rebranded other product lines, such as Marlboro Bright Leaf, to "Bright Leaf" to differentiate them from the main international Marlboro brand and maintain its premium status.
